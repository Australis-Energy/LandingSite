# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

UK renewable-energy professionals evaluating where to build — solar and wind
developers, investment/fund teams, and technical advisors running early-stage
due diligence for clients. Their context is a pipeline problem: dozens to
hundreds of candidate sites (the site copy references *"500+ sites for 2025
deployments"*), most of which will fail late for reasons that were knowable
early — grid headroom, land classification, planning policy, environmental
constraints. They arrive skeptical, time-poor, and fluent in the domain; they
are not looking to be taught what a constraint is, they are looking to trust a
verdict fast.

The job to be done: **turn a slow, manual, weeks-long site review into a
confident go/no-go in minutes** — and produce evidence (scores, constraint
maps, reports) they can hand to investors, consultants, or a planning process.

## Product Purpose

Australis (australis.energy) is AI-powered renewable-energy **site assessment**
software. It scores a site's *developability*, runs 22 constraint calculators
(grid, land, planning, environmental), and exports investor- and permit-ready
evidence. The public marketing site exists to convert domain-expert skeptics
into demo requests by **demonstrating decisiveness** — the hero is a live,
self-running mini-assessment (site boundary drawing itself, a developability
score counting up to 88, constraint rows resolving to CRITICAL/LOW/GOOD), not a
static screenshot. Success for this surface is a booked demo or waiting-list
signup from a qualified developer/investor. Company: Australis Technologies Ltd.
Positioning line, verbatim from the footer: *"De-Risking Your Pipeline.
Accelerating Deployment."*

## Brand Personality

**Precise · Confident · Fast.** The voice is an expert peer, not a vendor. It
speaks in the user's own terms (grid headroom, developability, go/no-go,
feasibility) and backs every claim with a number: *"In minutes, not months,"*
*"95% faster,"* *"22 constraint calculators,"* *"2 out of 3 planning
applications fail."* Sentences are short and imperative — *"Focus on what works.
Skip what doesn't."* The emotional target is **control and clarity**: the relief
of a hard decision made quickly and defensibly. Credibility is earned through
specificity and named backing (Ordnance Survey, Geovation, AWS), never through
adjectives. Optimistic and energetic, but never breathless or salesy.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI-tool marketing.** No Inter-plus-purple-gradient sameness, no
  emoji headlines, no "✨ Supercharge your workflow" filler. Australis has a
  hand-built identity; it must never regress to a template default.
- **Greenwashed clean-energy stock imagery.** No solar-panels-at-sunset photos,
  no leaf/globe logos, no wind-turbine hero shots. The product's proof is a
  *working assessment*, not a mood board. Show the tool, not the vibe.
- **Enterprise-GIS density.** Not ArcGIS/Esri: not a wall of gray panels,
  dropdowns, and coordinate readouts. The site sells a *verdict*, so it must
  feel decisive and legible, not like heavy surveying software.
- **Consumer dark-mode neon dashboards.** No glowing charts on near-black,
  crypto-style gradients, or aggressive contrast. The surface is light, calm,
  and precise.

## Design Principles

- **Demonstrate the verdict, don't describe it.** The strongest asset is a
  working mini-product (animated hero assessment, count-up stats, score ring).
  Show speed and confidence happening on screen rather than claiming it in prose.
- **Every visual maps to a real concept.** The blueprint grid reads as
  surveying; polygons are site boundaries; the aqua line is a grid connection;
  the ring is a developability score. Decoration must earn its place by meaning
  something in the domain — no ornament for its own sake.
- **Credibility through specificity.** Prefer a number, a named data source, or
  a named backer over an adjective. Concrete beats impressive.
- **Expert confidence, plainly stated.** Address a domain professional as a
  peer. Use their vocabulary without a jargon wall; lead with the outcome
  (find the best sites, skip the bad ones) before the mechanism.
- **Calm precision under motion.** Animation communicates the product working,
  not flourish. Choreography should feel engineered and purposeful, and must
  never get in the way of a skeptical reader scanning fast.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Two structural cautions specific to this system:

- **Motion.** The surface leans heavily on entrance animations, count-ups,
  floating chips, and a logo marquee. There is currently **no
  `prefers-reduced-motion` handling** — a known gap to close so motion-sensitive
  users get a static, still-legible experience.
- **Color is never the only signal.** Constraint status already pairs the
  traffic-light dots with text labels (CRITICAL / LOW / GOOD) — keep that.
  Note that brand **aqua (#3bf5b7) fails contrast as foreground text** and is
  deliberately restricted to accents/gradients; legible green text uses emerald
  instead. Maintain AA contrast for all body and label text against its
  background (navy-on-light, white-on-navy).
